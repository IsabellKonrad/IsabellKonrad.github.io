Songs of God

I wrote a webpage which provides over 200 christian songs with corresponding guitar tabs. Each song can be transposed to any mode on demand, using a RESTful API in Flask and Python. When new songs are added to the song pool the mode is automatically detected by a support vector machine that I wrote and trained in Scikit-learn.

Read Notes

Read Notes is a project to build a classifier, which recognizes whether an image file of a stave contains a note, using Google's new machine learning library TensorFlow. In several preprocessing steps the scanned complete musical pieces are separated into single staves, and the staves into single notes or other objects such as clefs, rests or bars. The current neural network classifier with three hidden layers detects a note with an accuracy of 80%.

Math Exam Recources

For years Math students at the University of British Columbia bought solved previous Math exams to study. These solved exams were copied prints of once solved and never reviewed questions and solutions. To improve the study condition and to open it to financially weaker students, I joined a group of graduate students who solved the exams again, published it online using MediaWiki and built an environment, where students find hints next to exam questions and solutions, can remark on questions or solution, find links to similar questions or can rate questions. The students also can search for questions by topic. Now, after developing the Math Exam Resources since Feb 2012, we have over 1500 fully solved questions including hints and solutions. By now our Wiki pages receive over 400.000 clicks per semester.

Wind turbine distance

This dynamical map of Lower Franconia uses the Google Maps API to display the location of wind turbines and their distances to residential areas. In 2013 the Bavarian state agency for environment suggested a minimal distance amounting 800m of wind turbines to residential areas. If the 10H-Idea becomes effective, the minimal distance would increase to 10 times the height of the wind turbine. Since the height of the wind turbines needs to be about 200m to be economical, the distance to residential areas exceeds about 2000m. Because discussion on the news seemed to be leaded without considering the geographical conditions, we developed the webpage Dynamical map, where the tabu zones for wind turbines are displayed in the region Lower Franconia, i.e. those areas where the distance of the wind turbines to residential domains deceeds the minimum. Using the dynamical map, it is noticeable that with a minimal distance of 2000m the tabu zones cover almost everything, where the remaining free areas mostly are reservation areas. Hence, the energy turnaround in Bavaria is not compatible with the 10H-Idea. To create the webpage Dynamical map we used Javascript, Unix shell and Python and embedded the map from Google.

Dice game

To learn how to programm computer games or apps, we implemented our favourite dice game based on the dice game Solow\"urfeln in Matlab. We published it on the webpage File Exchange belonging to Mathworks, where people share open source custom Matlab implementations. File Exchange (Dice Game by Bernhard Konrad). You can play against human or computer components.
The computer opponents, you can play against in the Matlab dice game, originate in the Dice game contest initiated by Bernhard Konrad and me. Besides us, two others participated in the contest and developed using different strategies four computer opponents which are incorporated in the Matlab dice game.

Viria

Based on the Ph.D. reseach topic of Bernhard Konrad, we thought up and made a board game for four players illustrating different szenarios in a body after HIV infection. Evidentially people learn easier by handling a subject self-acting and playfully. By playing the boardgame Viria people should become aware of the severity of an HIV infection.
